Introduction

This report analyzes the usage patterns of Cyclistic bike-share service by annual members and casual riders. The analysis aims to answer the following questions:

  1. How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

  2. Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?

  3. How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

Data Cleaning

# Data Cleaning
all_data <- all_data %>% 
  select(trip_id, start_at, end_at, bike_id, tripduration, start_station_id, start_station_name, 
         end_station_id, end_station_name, casual_member, ride_length, day_of_week)

all_data <- all_data %>% 
  drop_na()

all_data$start_at <- as.POSIXct(all_data$start_at, format = "%m/%d/%Y %H:%M:%S")
all_data$end_at <- as.POSIXct(all_data$end_at, format = "%m/%d/%Y %H:%M:%S")

# Convert ride_length from HH:MM:SS to total seconds
all_data$ride_length <- period_to_seconds(hms(all_data$ride_length))

Question 1: How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

Average Ride Duration by User Type

# Average ride Duration by User Type
avg_ride_duration <- all_data %>% 
  group_by(casual_member) %>% 
  summarise(avg_duration = mean(ride_length, na.rm = TRUE))

Summary for Question 1

  • Casual riders ride the bikes for, on average, longer bike trips.

  • There are more annual members using the bike service on a daily basis.

  • The top starting stations for members are: Canal St & Adams, Clinton St & Madison St, Clinton St & Washington Blvd, Kingsbury St & Kinzie St, and Columbus Dr & Randolph St.

  • The top starting stations for casual riders are: Streeter Dr & Grand Ave, Lake Shore Dr & Monroe St, Shedd Aquarium, Millenium Park, and Dusable Harbor.

  • More bikers, both casual and members, use the bike service in October the most, with November having slightly more riders than December, but with all 3 months, very low casual riders and high membership riders.

Conclusion

By leveraging insights from the data, Cyclistic can effectively encourage casual riders to become annual members. The analysis shows that casual riders tend to take longer trips and have specific peak usage times and popular starting stations. Digital media campaigns targeting these key insights can highlight the benefits of membership, such as cost savings and convenience. Personalized emails and social media engagement, along with strategic promotions at popular stations, can drive conversions from casual riders to annual members, enhancing their overall experience with Cyclistic.
